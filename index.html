Zone 2

Pots

Two railing pots (~10"×24" oval) for delicate herbs, plus one dedicated strawberry pot. Movable for sun/shade management.

🌿

Railing Pot 1 — Chamomile

Delicate, shallow-rooted. Place near beds — attracts hoverflies and parasitic wasps that patrol vegetables. Move to partial shade in peak July-August heat.

🌿

Railing Pot 2 — Sage

Hates wet feet — pot drainage is ideal. Place near Bed 4 to deter flea beetles from eggplant. Perennial in 8a — overwinters in pot with minimal protection.

🍓

Strawberry Pot — Strawberries

Full sun. Runners cascade over edges. Check moisture daily — pots dry fast in summer. Light fruit this year, heavy production next spring. Hardy in 8a with light crown mulch.

Technique

Trailing Vine Management

Squash and watermelon root in the beds but trail into the yard. Here’s how to manage the vine zone.

Prep the Landing Zone

Mow the yard in front of beds short. Lay cardboard or landscape fabric. Cover with straw mulch to suppress grass and prevent fruit rot.

Direct the Vines Weekly

Gently steer vines to avoid tangling. Keep pathways clear. Redirect any that head toward walkways or each other.

Protect Watermelon Fruit

Once fruit is golf-ball sized, slide cardboard or a small board underneath to prevent bottom rot from wet grass contact.

Mowing Blackout

The 6-8 ft zone in front of beds becomes vine territory from mid-June through harvest. Mow it short before planting, then mow around it all season.

Defense

Pest Resistance Strategy

Zone 8a’s milder winters mean more pests survive overwinter. Pressure starts earlier and lasts longer. Your garden has built-in biological defenses.

Threat Calendar

WhenPestTargetsAction
Early Jun Squash Vine Borer High All squash, watermelon Wrap stems with aluminum foil at base. Inspect weekly for frass. Inject Bt at first sign.
Jun–Jul Cucumber Beetle Med Cucumbers, squash, melon Row covers until flowering. Yellow sticky traps. Kaolin clay spray.
Jun–Aug Tomato Hornworm Med Tomatoes, peppers, eggplant Basil deters moths. Hand-pick. Leave caterpillars with white cocoons (braconid wasp eggs).
Jun–Sep Flea Beetle Med Eggplant Banana pepper companions help. Sage pot nearby. Row cover Gretel first 2-3 weeks if heavy.
Jul–Aug Japanese Beetle Med Many plants Hand-pick into soapy water AM. No bag traps. Milky spore in lawn for grubs.
Jul–Oct Stink Bugs Med Tomatoes, peppers Sunflowers as trap crop in front yard. Hand-pick into soapy water.
All season Aphids Low Peppers, lettuce, eggplant Basil and sage repel. Chamomile and black-eyed susan attract predators. Blast with hose.
Aug–Sep 2nd Wave Vine Borer High Late squash Continue stem inspections through September.

Care

Watering Guidelines

Zone 8a summers are hot and humid. Consistent watering prevents blossom end rot, bitter cucumbers, and wilting herbs.

PlantsNeedSchedule
Tomatoes, peppers, eggplant Moderate 1-2" per week. Deep water every 2-3 days. Mulch beds 2-3". Inconsistent watering causes blossom end rot.
Cucumbers High Water daily in peak summer. Mulch heavily. Never let dry out — drought causes bitter fruit.
Squash, watermelon Moderate Deep soak 2×/week at root crown. Water morning to keep foliage dry. Vines find supplemental moisture.
Lettuce Moderate Daily light watering. Once it bolts, pull it.
Basil Moderate Water when top inch of soil is dry. Don’t let it wilt.
Chamomile & sage (pots) Low Let soil dry slightly between waterings. Sage especially hates wet roots.
Strawberries (pot) High Check daily. May need water 2×/day in 90°F+ heat. Pots dry fast.
Rosemary (front yard) Low Weekly first month, then only during extended drought. Hates wet feet.
Front yard perennials Moderate 2-3×/week first summer to establish. Low-water after. Belarina needs consistent moisture permanently.
Schedule

Monthly Task Calendar

Key tasks for each month of the growing season and beyond.

Late May (Now)
  • Plant everything
  • Mulch all beds 2-3 inches deep
  • Prep vine landing zone with cardboard and straw
  • Set up trellises on fence for cucumbers and tomatoes
June
  • Harvest lettuce aggressively — pull when it bolts
  • Begin squash vine borer stem checks
  • Pinch tomato suckers to manage size
  • Train cucumber vines up fence trellis
  • Direct squash and melon vines weekly
  • Fertilize beds every 2-3 weeks
July
  • Peak harvest: banana peppers, cucumbers, squash
  • First Gretel eggplant harvest
  • Japanese beetle watch — hand-pick mornings
  • Stink bug watch — sunflowers drawing them
  • Deep water consistently through heat
August
  • Watermelon harvest (hollow thump = ripe)
  • Spaghetti squash harvest (hard skin, tan color)
  • Continue pepper and tomato harvest
  • Deadhead sunflowers for extended bloom
  • Second vine borer wave — inspect stems
September
  • Late tomato and pepper harvest continues
  • Pull spent cucumber vines
  • Clean up finished squash vines
  • Cut chamomile flowers for drying if desired
October
  • Final tomato and pepper harvest before frost
  • Leave perennials in place (rosemary, sage, heuchera, etc.)
  • Pull annual vegetable plants
November
  • First frost ~Nov 15
  • Mulch strawberry pot crown lightly
  • Cut Mexican sage to 6" after frost browns it
  • Compost spent annual plants
  • Mulch perennial beds for winter
February
  • Cut Mexican sage to 6" if not done in fall
  • Prune rosemary to shape
  • Light trim on sage in pot
  • Plan next year’s seed order
